# arXiv:2604.04263 — Commercial Persuasion in AI-Mediated Conversations

> Princeton preregistered RCT (N=2,012) showing conversational AI triples sponsored product selection rates; transparency labels statistically ineffective.

## Key Facts

- **Authors**: Francesco Salvi, Alejandro Cuevas, Manoel Horta Ribeiro — Princeton University (ref: sources/commercial-persuasion-ai-mediated-conversations.md)
- **Published**: April 7, 2026 (ref: sources/commercial-persuasion-ai-mediated-conversations.md)
- **Method**: Preregistered RCT, 2,012 U.S. eBook readers, 5 conditions, real product catalog (5,495 titles), real monetary incentive (book vs $1 cash) (ref: sources/commercial-persuasion-ai-mediated-conversations.md)
- **Models tested**: GPT-5.2, Claude Opus 4.5, Gemini 3 Pro, DeepSeek v3.2, Qwen3 235b — randomly assigned across participants (ref: sources/commercial-persuasion-ai-mediated-conversations.md)
- **Core result**: Active AI persuasion achieves 61.2% selection vs 22.4% neutral, 31.1% traditional search (ref: sources/commercial-persuasion-ai-mediated-conversations.md)
- **Transparency failure**: Sponsored labels + warnings reduce by 5.7pp (ns); covert persuasion detected by only 9.5% of users (ref: sources/commercial-persuasion-ai-mediated-conversations.md)
- **Mechanism**: 11-strategy taxonomy; disparagement strategies (Active Hedging, Understated Description) outperform promotion strategies (ref: sources/commercial-persuasion-ai-mediated-conversations.md)

## Relevance to STOPA

First large-scale causal evidence that AI persuasion is a different category from traditional advertising — not incremental improvement but structural shift. Informs STOPA agent design: system prompt commercial objectives + conversational interface = covert persuasion capability by default.
