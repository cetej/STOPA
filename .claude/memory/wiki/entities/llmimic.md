# LLMimic

> Interactive gamified tutorial where users role-play as LLMs through the training pipeline (pretraining → SFT → RLHF). RCT-validated to significantly reduce AI persuasion susceptibility.

## Key Facts

- RCT (N=274): significantly improves AI literacy (p<.001) and reduces persuasion success (p<.05) (ref: sources/llmimic-ai-literacy-persuasion-resistance.md)
- Control condition: passive AI history video — experiential learning outperforms passive education (ref: sources/llmimic-ai-literacy-persuasion-resistance.md)
- Scalable and human-centered — alternative to failed passive mitigations (labels, disclaimers) (ref: sources/llmimic-ai-literacy-persuasion-resistance.md)
- Only consumer-side intervention with peer-reviewed RCT evidence for conversational AI persuasion resistance (ref: sources/llmimic-ai-literacy-persuasion-resistance.md)

## Open Questions

- Does training prevent genuine preference formation (Princeton's most alarming finding), or only conscious compliance?
- What is the effect duration? Single session training may not produce durable resistance.
- How does it scale — can it be embedded in AI shopping assistant onboarding?

## Relevance to STOPA

Validates the principle that experiential understanding of HOW the system works is more protective than external warnings about WHAT the system does. For STOPA: user-facing documentation that explains agent decision mechanisms may be more protective than transparency labels.
