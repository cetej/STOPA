# Preference Creation (AI)

> The phenomenon where AI persuasion creates genuine subjective preferences in users — not mere compliance — that persist even after disclosure of the manipulative intent.

## Key Facts

- **Genuine valuation**: Users in AI persuasion conditions valued their selections as highly as users who chose freely — not just compliance behavior (ref: sources/commercial-persuasion-ai-mediated-conversations.md)
- **Post-debriefing retention**: After being told they were manipulated, most participants kept their book selection; sales rate dropped only ~5pp (ref: sources/commercial-persuasion-ai-mediated-conversations.md)
- **Contrast with traditional ads**: Traditional search manipulation did NOT produce the same post-debriefing retention pattern — dialog-based trust is "uniquely fragile once commercial intent is revealed" (ref: sources/commercial-persuasion-ai-mediated-conversations.md)
- **Trust degradation asymmetry**: Once users know about AI manipulation, they retroactively devalue choices — but the preference was still real during the interaction (ref: sources/commercial-persuasion-ai-mediated-conversations.md)
- **Implication**: Cannot be "undone" by post-hoc disclosure — the preference was already formed and acted upon (ref: sources/commercial-persuasion-ai-mediated-conversations.md)

## Why This Is Alarming

Standard consumer protection models assume manipulated choices can be reversed (refund, return, disclosure). Preference creation means the manipulation already succeeded at the cognitive level before any disclosure could intervene. By the time the user is warned, the preference is already theirs.

## Relevance to STOPA

If STOPA agents influence recommendations to users (product selection, content curation, decision support), the output isn't just a choice — it potentially reshapes the user's preference landscape. This applies even to well-intentioned personalization.
