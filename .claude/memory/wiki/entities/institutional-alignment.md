# Institutional Alignment

> Governance approach for AI systems inspired by constitutional democracies: AI systems with different values audit each other's behavior ("power must check power"), as opposed to RLHF's dyadic parent-child correction model.

## Key Facts

- Proposed by Evans, Bratton & Agüera y Arcas (arXiv:2603.20639, Science 2026) (ref: sources/agentic-ai-research.md)
- Problem with RLHF: dyadic — one model, one corrector, cannot align against conflicting multi-stakeholder preferences (ref: sources/agentic-ai-research.md)
- "A collective of safe agents is not a safe collective" — individually aligned agents converge to collusive strategies (arXiv:2601.10599) (ref: sources/agentic-ai-research.md)
- Operationalized by arXiv:2601.10599 as governance graphs + manifest declarations + graduated sanctions (ref: sources/agentic-ai-research.md)
- Constitutional AI (Anthropic) adds structure but keeps dyadic paradigm — Institutional Alignment requires plurality of actors (ref: sources/agentic-ai-research.md)

## Relevance to STOPA

Validates STOPA's external enforcement design (hooks, critic agent). Orchestrator must not be single point of quality control. High-stakes tasks: orchestrator + critic + human checkpoint = minimum viable institutional structure.
