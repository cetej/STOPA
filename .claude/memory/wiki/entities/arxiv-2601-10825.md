# arXiv:2601.10825 — Reasoning Models Generate Societies of Thought

> Empirical study demonstrating that reasoning models spontaneously generate internal multi-perspective debate on 8,262 tasks across 6 benchmarks, without explicit multi-agent training.

## Key Facts

- Models tested: DeepSeek-R1 (671B), QwQ-32B vs 4 instruction-tuned baselines (ref: sources/agentic-ai-research.md)
- Benchmarks: BigBench Hard, GPQA, MATH Hard, MMLU-Pro, MUSR, IFEval (ref: sources/agentic-ai-research.md)
- RL fine-tuning on multi-agent dialogue: 38% accuracy @step40 vs 28% monologue (ref: sources/agentic-ai-research.md)
- SAE Feature 30939: steering it doubled arithmetic precision; SEM proved causal relationship (ref: sources/agentic-ai-research.md)
- Primary empirical basis for Evans et al. arXiv:2603.20639 (ref: sources/agentic-ai-research.md)

## Relevance to STOPA

Justifies heterogeneous agent system prompts in STOPA. Sonnet reasoning capability benefits from diverse "internal debate" — this happens naturally but can be amplified by critic agents with divergent prompts.
