## Summary

Karpathy argues there is a growing perception gap about AI capabilities driven by two factors. First, many people formed views from outdated free-tier ChatGPT and haven't updated to reflect current agentic frontier models (Claude Code, OpenAI Codex). Second, AI capabilities are "peaky" — dramatically advanced in verifiable technical domains (programming, math) but not comparably improved for general tasks (search, writing, advice), partly because general tasks lack clear reward functions and commercial incentives.

## Key Concepts

- AI capability perception gap: two groups talking past each other
- Peaky capabilities: huge gains in programming/math, modest in general tasks
- Reward function availability → commercial incentive → capability concentration
- Casual users (free-tier ChatGPT) vs professional devs (agentic frontier models)
- Published April 18, 2026

## Quote

Paraphrase: "Two people can both be right about AI — one seeing transformative improvements in code, another seeing failures in general tasks. They're using different tools at different capability tiers."
